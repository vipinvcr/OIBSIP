CAPTAIN VIKRAM BATRA (1974-1999)
KNOWN FOR : His selfless actions and valour during the kargil war 1999
DATE OF BIRTH: 9 September 1974
BIRTH PLACE : Palampur, Himachal Pradesh
AWARDS: Param Vir Chakra (The highest Indian military decoration)
The following list is a time line of Capt. Batra's Life
  • 1974 : Born in Palampur (a hill station in Kangra district), Himachal Pradesh(India) on 9 September 1974
  • 1990 : Represented his school in table tennis at All India KVS Nationals
  • 1992 : Completed 12th board examinations and started pursuing BSc Medical Sciences from DAV College, Chandigarh
  • 1992 : Joined NCC (National Cadet Crops) while he was in his first year of college, was adjudged the best NCC Air Wing cadet of Punjab Directorate in North Zone, later became the SUO (Senior Under Officer)
  • 1994 : Selected and took part in the Republic Day parade as an NCC cadet, thereafter decided to join the Indian Army
  • 1995 : Selected for the merchant navy at a shipping company headquartered in Hong Kong, but ultimately changed his mind, aspiring to do "something great, something extraordinary, which may bring fame to the country
  • 1995 : Enrolled at Panjab University in Chandigarh, took admission in MA English course, started preparing for the Combined Defence Services (CDS) Examination
  • 1996 : Joined Indian Military Academy (IMA)
  • 1997 : Commissioned as a lieutenant into the 13th battalion of the Jammu and Kashmir Rifles (13 JAK Rif) of the Indian Army
  • 1999 : Rose to the rank of Captain
  • 1999 : Was hit by a sniper from very close range, following which he was hit by a RPG on head, as a result, Capt. Batra succumbed to the fatal wounds
  • 1999 : Was awarded the Param Vir Chakra, India's highest military honour on 15 August 1999
